Emotion & Image Phase 02 · Cinematographic Language
The Unbreakable Principle
Core Law
Every technical decision — lens choice, camera movement, depth of field, cut timing — must serve the emotional target defined in the brief. If you cannot articulate why a choice serves the feeling, it's decoration.

Camera Movement Language
Static / Locked
World acts on subject. Patience. Time passing.
Slow Push
Growing importance. Intimacy accumulating.
Pull / Withdraw
Loss. Context reveal. Elegiac distance.
Handheld
Presence, aliveness. Implies a body behind the camera.
Pan / Tilt
Connection between things. Revelation.
Tracking / Follow
Accompaniment. Solidarity or pursuit.
Exposure Philosophy
Intentional Underexposure
  • Shadow detail less important than mood
  • Subject weight, gravity, withdrawal
  • Night or low-light as environment
Intentional Overexposure
  • Memory, dream, imagination
  • Grief, dissociation
  • Summer / heat / overwhelm
Depth of Field Strategy
Shallow DoF
Isolates subject from world. Danger: aestheticizes everything equally.
Deep DoF
Subject exists in world. Documentary honesty. Demands compositional rigor.

Film Theory Phase 04 · Structural & Narrative Principles
Editing Grammar
Continuity Cutting
Invisible edits. Match on action, eyeline, screen direction.
Jump Cut
Time ellision. Urgency, Godard distance, fragmentation.
Montage
Meaning from collision. Eisensteinian: conflict creates concept.
Long Take
Time as subject. Real duration creates weight, presence.
L-Cut / J-Cut
Audio leads or lags image. Anticipation or resonance.
Match Cut
Graphic or conceptual rhyme. Transfers meaning.
